The Challenge

The controller was operating as AP clerk, reconciliation analyst, and close manager simultaneously. Month-end closes routinely stretched past the board-reporting deadline, and management reporting was arriving 18 days after quarter-end — too late to act on.

1

Month-end close was missing the board reporting window

The standard 12-business-day close cycle meant management accounts reached the board two weeks after quarter close. Strategic decisions were being made on stale numbers, and the auditor was flagging the close process itself as a material weakness in internal controls.

2

Reconciliation backlog was compounding every month

With 240+ accounts requiring reconciliation across three entities and two currencies, the in-house team was closing ~70% on time and rolling the remaining 30% forward. Cumulative unreconciled balances reached $3.8M before the firm accepted the model was structurally broken.

3

Local hiring economics didn't close the gap

A mid-level US senior accountant cost $85K–$115K fully loaded with a 10–12 week hiring cycle. To run parallel AP, AR, reconciliation and reporting functions, the firm needed 5–6 hires — roughly $520K annual payroll before benefits, unworkable against the finance budget.
