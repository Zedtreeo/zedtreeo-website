The Challenge

The firm specializes in growth strategy and market-entry engagements for mid-market PE portfolio companies and corporate development teams. With 40+ active engagements and a 55-person consulting bench, the research-to-deck pipeline had become the single biggest constraint on revenue capacity. Partners were turning down $2.4M in proposal opportunities per quarter because the research team couldn’t produce competitive intelligence, financial models, and client-ready decks fast enough to meet proposal timelines.

1

Deck turnaround was killing proposal win rates

Average time from research brief to client-ready deck was 18 business days. Competing firms were delivering in 5–7 days. The firm’s proposal win rate had dropped from 34% to 21% over 12 months, and three long-standing PE clients cited “slow deliverables” in their annual vendor reviews. Partners estimated $2.4M in lost proposals per quarter directly attributable to research bottlenecks.

2

Senior consultants were doing junior analyst work

With only 2 research analysts supporting 55 consultants, senior associates and principals were spending 35% of their time on data pulls from PitchBook, Capital IQ, and Statista — work that billed at $0 internally but displaced $380/hour client-facing advisory time. The firm was losing $1.1M annually in unbilled senior capacity consumed by research tasks.

3

Freelance analysts couldn’t match quality standards

The firm cycled through 8 freelance research analysts in 14 months at $125–$185/hour. Deliverables consistently failed the firm’s formatting standards — wrong chart types, inconsistent color palettes, misaligned data labels. Partners spent 4–6 hours reformatting every deck before client delivery. Two client presentations contained data errors from freelancer work that required formal correction letters.

4

Hiring junior analysts locally was slow and expensive

A US-based research analyst with PitchBook/Capital IQ proficiency costs $85K–$115K fully loaded with a 12–16 week hiring cycle. To build a research bench that could support 40+ engagements, the firm needed 4–5 analysts — roughly $460K in annual payroll — and the last two job postings on LinkedIn attracted only 6 qualified applicants in 45 days.
