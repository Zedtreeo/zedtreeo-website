The Challenge

As the firm's project pipeline grew across commercial and high-end residential work, their visualization capacity became the single biggest bottleneck between schematic design and client sign-off. Every proposal, every design iteration, every client pitch queued behind a small, overloaded render team.

1

Render delays were stalling proposals

With only two in-house 3D artists handling a pipeline of 30+ active projects, turnaround on photoreal stills and walkthroughs stretched to 10–14 days. Business development leaders were missing proposal windows, and client pitches went out with placeholder line-work instead of the final renders that typically drive sign-off.

2

Freelancers introduced quality drift

The team had rotated through eight different freelance renderers over 18 months. Output quality swung dramatically, brand-compliant lighting setups were inconsistent, and scene files had to be cleaned by senior architects before any client delivery — compounding the bottleneck rather than relieving it.

3

Local hiring economics didn't work

A mid-level US 3D visualizer cost $78K–$95K fully loaded with a 10–12 week hiring cycle. To double their throughput the firm needed 2–3 new hires, representing roughly $250K in annual payroll before benefits — unworkable against project-level visualization budgets.
