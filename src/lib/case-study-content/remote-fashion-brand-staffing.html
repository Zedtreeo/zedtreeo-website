The Challenge

The brand operates a direct-to-consumer model with monthly product line updates, each requiring a coordinated launch across Shopify, email (Klaviyo), social media (Instagram + TikTok), and paid ads. With a 3-person in-house team handling everything from design to customer queries, the founder was personally approving assets at midnight and customer DMs were going unanswered for 8+ hours during UK off-peak periods. Two consecutive product launches missed their drop dates, costing an estimated £38K in pre-order momentum.

1

Creative bottleneck was delaying every launch

A single in-house designer was responsible for all campaign visuals — social posts, email banners, Shopify collection pages, paid ad creatives, and influencer kits. Average campaign cycle ran 4 weeks from brief to launch. Competitors with dedicated creative teams were shipping in 10 days, capturing first-mover advantage on seasonal trends worth £15K–£25K per drop.

2

Customer support gaps were hurting retention

Gorgias tickets averaged a 6-hour first-response time, with Instagram and TikTok DMs taking 11 hours. Post-purchase CSAT dropped to 3.4/5. Return-customer rate fell from 42% to 31% in two quarters. The brand had no support coverage between 6 PM and 8 AM GMT — exactly when 44% of US and global customers were browsing and buying.

3

Freelancer costs were unsustainable with no brand consistency

The brand cycled through 7 freelancers in 12 months — designers, copywriters, social managers — spending £11K/month with no guarantee of brand-voice alignment. Three separate freelancers produced three different visual identities in a single quarter. UK-based full-time hires for each role would cost £145K+ in annual salary alone, well beyond the operating budget.
