The Challenge

The platform hosts a UGC marketplace with 12 million monthly active users generating 380,000+ pieces of content daily — images, video, text posts, and marketplace listings. A 4-person in-house moderation team covered 10 AM–8 PM ET, leaving overnight and weekend queues unreviewed for 14+ hours. The EU Digital Services Act timeline was six months out, CSAM reporting obligations were tightening, and the most recent brand-safety incident — a hate-speech cluster that went viral before moderators saw it — cost the platform two mid-tier advertising partners worth $1.8M in combined annual revenue.

1

Queue backlogs were creating regulatory and brand risk

Average time-to-first-review had stretched to 11.4 hours. During overnight and weekend windows, the queue accumulated 26,000+ unreviewed items. Two DSA transparency report deadlines were approaching, and the legal team flagged that the platform’s current review cadence would fail the “expeditious removal” threshold for illegal content under Article 16.

2

Moderator burnout was destroying retention and quality

The 4-person in-house team was turning over at 40% per quarter. Each departing moderator cost $18K in recruiting and retraining. Accuracy on Tier-2 escalations had dropped to 71% as fatigued reviewers rushed through graphic-content queues. Two moderators filed HR complaints citing inadequate wellness support for CSAM and self-harm exposure.

3

Scaling locally was cost-prohibitive and slow

A US-based content moderator costs $52K–$68K fully loaded with a 10–14 week hiring cycle. To achieve 18/7 coverage with proper shift rotation and wellness breaks, the platform needed 8–10 moderators — roughly $520K in annual payroll — and the trust-and-safety budget was capped at $180K for the fiscal year.

4

AI-only moderation was generating unacceptable false positives

The platform had deployed Hive Moderation and AWS Rekognition for automated first-pass filtering, but 23% of auto-flagged content were false positives — legitimate creator posts being suppressed. Creator complaints about wrongful takedowns increased 180% in Q3, and three top creators with combined 2.1M followers publicly threatened to leave the platform.
