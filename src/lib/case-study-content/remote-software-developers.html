The Challenge

Post-Series-B, the firm had the roadmap but not the bench. US engineering hiring was running at a 10-month average time-to-productive, and the board had set a burn-multiple target that ruled out the obvious fix of simply hiring 12 more senior engineers.

1

Roadmap debt was compounding against the next round

Investor-visible shipping metrics were slipping. The firm had committed to 6 major feature releases in 12 months and was tracking at 2.5 on baseline velocity — a gap that was going to become the dominant narrative at the Series C.

2

US hiring couldn't keep pace with the board plan

A senior US engineer cost $180K–$240K fully loaded with a 10-month ramp to productive output. To meet the roadmap the firm needed 10–12 new engineers inside six months — structurally impossible at US-market compensation and time-to-hire.

3

Staff augmentation wasn't solving it either

Two previous agency engagements had delivered contractor-style throughput but no continuity, no ownership, and no documented code. Every feature shipped by an agency became maintenance debt for the in-house team, and net velocity went backward within a quarter.
